8 BioBLU® 0.3c/f single-use bioreactor
English (EN)
2.3 Product features
2.3.1 Overview
The BioBLU 0.3c/f single-use bioreactor is a full-featured plastic bioreactor for single use.
All connection options for sensors, liquid media and gassing are already integrated in the head plate. No
additional parts are required.
Critical process parameters such as pH, DO and temperature can be monitored and controlled using inte-
grated industry standard probes. A sterile sampling valve enables needle-free sampling.
With a working volume range of 100 – 250 mL, respectively 65 – 250 mL the BioBLU 0.3c/f is ideally suited
for use in the early stages of process development.
For applications in the fields of cell culture and microbiology, two versions of the BioBLU 0.3c/f are avail-
able.
The BioBLU 0.3c/f single-use bioreactor has been specifically designed for use with the DASbox parallel
bioreactor system. This allows the advantages of parallel working to be combined with single-use technol-
ogy.
2.3.2 Single-use technology
Single-use technology is becoming increasingly important especially in the cultivation of mammalian cell
cultures. It is considered to be a time-saving and cost-effective alternative to the use of traditional reusable
bioreactors.
The head plate of the single-use bioreactor is an injection molded part in which main connecting parts and
internal fixtures are already fully integrated. Other internal fittings and the vessel body are welded to the
head plate.
The bioreactor is delivered completely assembled. Eliminating the need to assemble and clean parts means
valuable time can be saved.
2.3.3 Sterility
The single-use bioreactor is delivered pre-sterilized and is ready for immediate use when removed from
packing. Sterilization is performed by irradiation.
Each single-use bioreactor is delivered in a sterile double packing.
2.3.4 Overhead drive
The agitation of the single-use bioreactor is achieved via a reusable DASbox overhead drive for single-use
bioreactors, which is connected to the stirrer rod of the bioreactor by magnetic coupling.
With a speed range from 20 to 2000 rpm, this stirring drive is equally suitable for applications in cell cul-
ture and microbiology.

BioBLU® 0.3c/f single-use bioreactor 9
English (EN)
2.3.5 Gas supply and exhaust condenser
For supplying gas to cell culture applications, the single-use bioreactor is equipped with two gas ports. The
gas mixture can either be introduced via an overlay inlet into the headspace of the bioreactor or routed
directly into the medium (gas overlay or gas submerged).
The exhaust gas line of the single-use bioreactor can be equipped with a reusable off-gas condenser. The
condensation of liquid in the exhaust gas significantly reduces the effects of evaporation. This avoids
potential negative effects that can occur due to variation of the medium osmolarity. The exhaust gas filter is
also prevented from becoming blocked.
Peltier-element-technology makes the use of coolants and coolant distributors unnecessary, thus allowing
easy handling of the exhaust condenser.
2.3.6 Monitoring and control of key process parameters
DO
A miniaturized Clark sensor (DO sensor DASGIP D 4.7 mm, L 162 mm) is used for the non-invasive mea-
surement of the DO. The head plate of the single-use bioreactor is equipped with a sensor port in which the
DO sensor can be integrated. The measuring chamber of the sensor port is separated from the culture
media by a permeable membrane (refer to chapter 4.1.2).
pH
Standard glass electrodes (120/12 mm) are used for measuring the pH value.
They are calibrated and sterilized before they are integrated into the single-use bioreactor via a Pg 13.5
port.
Temperature
A Pt100 RTD temperature sensor is used for measuring the temperature.

Eppendorf BioBLU Single-Use Bioreactor
The BioBLU Single-Use Bioreactor (part numbers listed see BioBLU part numbers e-beam on page 33 and

 Avoid contact if you wear a pacemaker or other similar equipment.

 During operation, also visually inspect the ports from time to time.
 Do not disconnect the connections during a process.

BioBLU® 0.3c/f single-use bioreactor 13
English (EN)
CAUTION! Damage to the single-use bioreactors caused by too high operating
temperatures.
The plastic material of the bioreactors has only limited temperature resistance. Operation at
high temperatures can lead to brittleness.
The material melts at temperatures above 80 °C.
A release of microorganisms is possible.
 Observe the maximum operating temperatures.
 Do not fill the bioreactor with hot liquids.
 The bioreactor is not rated for use post autoclave. The bioreactor may deform and lose
integrity when autoclaved.
 Observe the maximum contact temperature, e.g. for heat blankets or the wells of the
temperature control unit.
The heating strategy must not deliver higher temperatures.
 Observe the limits given in the chapter "Application limits".
NOTICE! Limited temperature resistance of single-use bioreactors. Observe maximum
contact temperature when setting temperature control.
The maximum contact temperature, e.g. for heat blankets or the wells of the temperature
control unit is 60 °C.
 Observe the following points when setting the temperature control with a DASGIP
temperature control unit (TCSC). This does not apply to DASbox systems.
• The maximum temperature of heat blankets or the wells of the DASGIP temperature
control unit can clearly exceed the adjusted setpoint (SP) of the process temperature.
• The maximum permissible temperature difference between heat blanket or wells of the
temperature control unit and the process temperature is set via the parameter "band" [K]
in the parameter menu of the TCSC module.
• The sum of the set point (SP) and the "band" must not exceed the maximum temperature
of 60 °C.
NOTICE! Sterility and risk of contamination.
The single-use bioreactor is delivered in sterile double packing.
 Only open the sterile packing shortly before using the bioreactor (4.2.2).
 Open the sterile packing under the sterile workbench or cleanbench.
NOTICE! Possible damage to bioreactor.
 Never activate temperature control without first plugging in the RTD and inserting it into
the thermowell inside the bioreactor.
 Only activate the temperature control after the medium is placed in the bioreactor.

 Protect the single-use bioreactor from permanent direct solar radiation.
 Store the single-use bioreactors in the original packing.

4.3 Troubleshooting
4.3.1 Adjusting the magnetic coupling of the overhead drive
The magnetic adapter of the overhead drive is adjustable in
height.
If the height is adjusted incorrectly, it will cause disruptions
during stirring operation.
Note
The height is usually adjusted at the factory before shipment and optimized for operation with
the single-use bioreactor.
Readjustments are usually not necessary.
Readjust the height if:
• the magnetic core audibly grinds on the plastic coupling of the single-use bioreactor and this results in
significant plastic wear.
In this case, the magnet is set too low.
• the magnetic coupling is repeatedly interrupted during stirring operation and the desired stirring
performance is not achieved.
In this case, the magnet may be set too high.
 Loosen the set screw for fixing the magnetic adapter with a
1.5 mm Allen key.
The height of the magnet adapter can now be adjusted.
 Select a minimum distance in order to ensure optimal
magnetic coupling.
 The adapter must not rub against the plastic coupling of the
bioreactor.
 Fix the set screw after successful adjustment.
